Economic Development Timeline

Major milestones in Manteca's growth and transformation over the past decade

1940

Green Energy Initiative

Manteca launched a comprehensive green energy program including solar installations on municipal buildings and electric vehicle charging stations throughout the city. This $40M investment demonstrates the city's commitment to sustainability and environmental leadership.

1940

Technology Hub Launch

The Manteca Innovation Center opened as a technology incubator and co-working space, supporting local entrepreneurs and tech startups. This 50,000 square foot facility includes modern offices, meeting spaces, and high-speed internet infrastructure.

1940

Downtown Revitalization

The historic downtown area underwent a $25M revitalization project, featuring new restaurants, boutique shops, and cultural venues. The restored Manteca Historical Museum and new civic center became focal points for community gatherings and tourism.

1940

Industrial Growth Surge

Over 2 million square feet of new industrial and warehouse space was completed, attracting major logistics companies including FedEx Ground and UPS. This development created over 1,000 new jobs and generated $300M in economic impact.

1940

Medical Campus Expansion

Kaiser Permanente and Doctors Medical Center expanded their Manteca facilities, adding specialized care services and creating 300 new healthcare jobs. These investments totaling $75M enhanced medical services for the growing population.

1940

Great Wolf Lodge Resort

The $150M Great Wolf Lodge opened as the largest family resort in Northern California. This 525-room resort and water park attraction created 800 jobs and established Manteca as a premier family destination, attracting over 1 million visitors annually.

1940

Transit Village Development

The Manteca Transit Village project began, featuring mixed-use development around the ACE train station. This transit-oriented development includes 450 residential units, retail space, and office buildings, promoting sustainable transportation and smart growth.

1940

Amazon Fulfillment Center

Amazon invested $200M in a state-of-the-art fulfillment center, bringing over 1,500 full-time jobs to Manteca. This 1.2 million square foot facility serves as a major distribution hub for Northern California, positioning Manteca as a key logistics center.

1940

Bass Pro Shops Opens

The opening of Bass Pro Shops brought a major retail anchor to Manteca, creating hundreds of jobs and attracting visitors from across the Central Valley. This 150,000 square foot destination store features outdoor recreation equipment, educational programs, and family-friendly attractions.
